Our Story

How Socrates came to be

In early 2025, our founders were frustrated. They were builders — developers, researchers, designers — who kept running into the same wall: incredible AI models existed, but turning them into usable tools required months of engineering work and thousands of dollars in infrastructure. Meanwhile, the tools that did exist were scattered across hundreds of disconnected platforms, each with their own onboarding, billing, and APIs.

There was no place where all AI lived. No hub. No standard. No marketplace where a non-technical person could discover and run a state-of-the-art AI tool in one click, and where the builder who created it could actually earn a living.

So we built it. Socrates launched in beta in January 2026 with 50 hand-picked builders and 500 tools. Within 30 days, it had grown to 10,000 tools and 200,000 users entirely through word of mouth. We knew then that we'd found something real.

Today, Socrates is the largest open AI marketplace in the world. We're just getting started.

What We Believe

Our values

🌍
Radical Access
The best AI should be a click away for anyone on earth. We eliminate every unnecessary barrier — signup friction, installation, cost, and technical complexity.
🔨
Builders First
The people who create the tools that others use should be rewarded generously for their work. 80% is not a marketing line — it's a commitment.
🛡️
Safety Without Compromise
Powerful AI in the wrong hands is dangerous. We run safety checks that would make most AI companies embarrassed by comparison — and we improve them every week.
📖
Quality Over Quantity
Trending is earned, not bought. Our ranking algorithm rewards tools that users actually love — measured by re-run rate, copy rate, and satisfaction, not clicks.
🤝
Honest Business
Simple pricing. No dark patterns. No burying your cancellation button. We keep 20% of what you earn, and we work hard to make that 20% worth it.
🧠
Curiosity Above All
Socrates didn't have answers. He had better questions. We build this platform in that spirit — curious, open, and convinced that wisdom comes from everywhere.
